P3 S1The randomised control trial involved hundreds of year 5 pupils—aged nine and ten—at 45 state primary schools that had not been “previously exposed to RSC pedagogy.” S2They were split into target and control groups and asked to write, for example, a message in a bottle as Ferdinand following the shipwreck in The Tempest. P4 S1The peer-reviewed results showed that the target group of pupils drew on a wider vocabulary, used words “classed as more sophisticated or rarer”, and wrote at greater length. S2They also “appear to be more comfortable writing in role... while [control] pupils imagine how they themselves would react to being shipwrecked, [target] children put themselves in the shoes of a literary character and express that character’s emotion”. S3The Time to Act study, which is published by the RSC this week, also found that while control pupils relied on “desert island clichés” such as palm trees, target pupils were “more expansive [giving] a broader picture of the sky, the sea and the atmospheric conditions”.

P5 S1O’Hanlon said she had been most surprised by the “emotional literacy” that was evident in the [target] children’s writing and that they were “more resilient in their writing, more hopeful”. S2She added “The emotional understanding was very evident and it is probably related to the [rehearsal room process] where you are used to trying to imagine your way through. They were comfortable in describing different emotional states and part of what you do in drama is put yourself in different shoes.” S3The study showed the importance of embedding arts in education, she said.

P6S1But could the results be replicated with any old dramatist? S2O’Hanlon said more research would be needed but suggested that Shakespeare’s use of 20,000 words, compared with the everyday 2,000 words, gave a “massive expansion of language into children’s lives”, which was combined with children “using their whole bodies to bring words to life”.

Q21细节理解题
正确答案 C

The “rehearsal room” approach requires pupils to

  • Arewrite the lines from Shakespeare.
  • Bwatch RSC actors’ performances.
  • Cplay the roles in Shakespeare.
  • Dstudy drama under RSC artists.
关键词题干关键词：“rehearsal room” approach；原文对应：act him out / drama-based activity / writing in role。
定位先由 P2 的 “rehearsal room approach” 定位，再顺读 P1、P3、P4 对该方法的解释。
证据P1 说 “act him out”，P3 说目标组配合文本做 drama-based activity，P4 说学生 writing in role。三处共同指向“扮演角色”。
替换play the roles = act him out / drama-based activity / writing in role。

干扰项反证

A原文要求写作任务，但不是“改写莎士比亚台词”。
Bactors work 是方法来源，不是让学生观看演员表演。
DRSC 设计/委托研究，但学生并非在 RSC 艺术家指导下系统学习戏剧。
Q22实验目的题
正确答案 A

The study divided the pupils into two groups to find whether

  • Athe change in instruction enhances learning outcomes.
  • Bexpanding vocabulary helps develop reading fluency.
  • Cemotion affects understanding of sophisticated works.
  • Dthe classroom activity stimulates interest in the arts.
关键词题干关键词：divided into two groups；原文对应：split into target and control groups；结果词：wider vocabulary / sophisticated / greater length / emotional literacy。
定位P3 出现分组；P4 给出目标组与控制组的结果差异，因此顺序切片为 P3-P4。
证据研究把学生分成目标组和控制组，目标组接受戏剧活动；结果显示目标组词汇更广、词更高级、写得更长，还更能进入角色表达情绪。
替换change in instruction = 30-minute drama-based activity；enhances learning outcomes = broadened vocabulary / complexity of writing / emotional literacy。

干扰项反证

B文中讲 vocabulary 和 writing，但没有说 reading fluency。
C文中讲情感素养，但不是研究“情绪是否影响理解复杂作品”。
D文中说 arts in education 重要，但没有用“兴趣提升”作为实验目标或结果。
