完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色加粗 marker = 题干关键词或原文同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。主旨题证据可分布在多个段落。

P1 S1In order to “change lives for the better” and reduce “dependency”, George Osborne, Chancellor of the Exchequer, introduced the “upfront work search” scheme. S2Only if the jobless arrive at the jobcentre with a CV, register for online job search, and start looking for work will they be eligible for benefit — and then they should report weekly rather than fortnightly. S3What could be more reasonable?

P2 S1More apparent reasonableness followed. S2There will now be a seven-day wait for the jobseeker’s allowance. S3“Those first few days should be spent looking for work, not looking to sign on,” he claimed. S4“We’re doing these things because we know they help people stay off benefits and help those on benefits get into work faster.” S5Help? Really? S6On first hearing, this was the socially concerned chancellor, trying to change lives for the better, complete with “reforms” to an obviously indulgent system that demands too little effort from the newly unemployed to find work, and subsidises laziness. S7What motivated him, we were to understand, was his zeal for “fundamental fairness” — protecting the taxpayer, controlling spending and ensuring that only the most deserving claimants received their benefits.

P3 S1Losing a job is hurting: you don’t skip down to the jobcentre with a song in your heart, delighted at the prospect of doubling your income from the generous state. S2It is financially terrifying, psychologically embarrassing and you know that support is minimal and extraordinarily hard to get. S3You are now not wanted; you are now excluded from the work environment that offers purpose and structure in your life. S4Worse, the crucial income to feed yourself and your family and pay the bills has disappeared. S5Ask anyone newly unemployed what they want and the answer is always: a job.

P4 S1But in Osborneland, your first instinct is to fall into dependency — permanent dependency if you can get it — supported by a state only too ready to indulge your falsehood. S2It is as though 20 years of ever-tougher reforms of the job search and benefit administration system never happened. S3The principle of British welfare is no longer that you can insure yourself against the risk of unemployment and receive unconditional payments if the disaster happens. S4Even the very phrase “jobseeker’s allowance” is about redefining the unemployed as a “jobseeker” who had no mandatory right to a benefit he or she has earned through making national insurance contributions. S5Instead, the claimant receives a time-limited “allowance,” conditional on actively seeking a job; no entitlement and no insurance, at £71.70 a week, one of the least generous in the EU.

Q21细节题
答案 A

21. George Osborne’s scheme was intended to

  • Aencourage jobseekers’ active engagement in job seeking.
  • Bprovide the unemployed with easier access to benefits.
  • Cguarantee jobseekers’ legitimate right to benefits.
  • Dmotivate the unemployed to report voluntarily.
关键词scheme / upfront work search → 原文首段的条件：CV、online job search、start looking for work。
定位定位 P1。题干 scheme 原词复现，后一句用 only if 列出领取福利前必须主动完成的求职动作。
证据只有带简历、注册网上求职、开始找工作才有资格领取福利，核心意图是促使求职者先主动找工作。
替换encourage active engagement = arrive with a CV + register + start looking for work。

干扰项反证

B原文设置了更多条件，不是让福利更容易获得。
C末段还说 no mandatory right，不能说保证合法权利。
Dreport weekly 只是附带要求，且不是 voluntary。
Q22词义题
答案 C

22. The phrase “to sign on” (Line 3, Para. 2) most probably means

  • Ato check on the availability of jobs at the jobcentre.
  • Bto accept the government’s restrictions on the allowance.
  • Cto register for an allowance from the government.
  • Dto attend a governmental job-training program.
关键词to sign on → seven-day wait for the jobseeker’s allowance。
定位定位 P2。该短语出现在 jobseeker’s allowance 语境中，与 looking for work 对比。
证据政府说前几天应花在找工作上，而不是 looking to sign on；sign on 指去登记领取津贴。
替换sign on = register for an allowance from the government。

干扰项反证

A找工作机会是 looking for work，不是 sign on。
Baccept restrictions 是对政策的接受，非短语含义。
D文中未提政府培训项目。
Q23细节题
答案 D

23. What prompted the chancellor to develop his scheme?

  • AA desire to secure a better life for all.
  • BAn eagerness to protect the unemployed.
  • CAn urge to be generous to the claimants.
  • DA passion to ensure fairness for taxpayers.
关键词prompted / motivated → What motivated him... fundamental fairness。
定位定位 P2 末句。题干 prompted 与原文 motivated 同义。
证据原文说其动机被解释为“fundamental fairness”：保护纳税人、控制支出、确保只有最应得者领福利。
替换a passion to ensure fairness for taxpayers = zeal for fundamental fairness / protecting the taxpayer。

干扰项反证

Achange lives for the better 是表层口号，后文指出真正动机是 taxpayer fairness。
B保护失业者与原文 controlling spending、deserving claimants 不符。
Cgenerous 与原文 least generous、controlling spending 相反。
Q24细节题
答案 A

24. According to Paragraph 3, being unemployed makes one feel

  • Auneasy.
  • Binsulted.
  • Cenraged.
  • Dguilty.
关键词being unemployed → Losing a job；feel → terrifying / embarrassing / not wanted。
定位定位 P3，段首 Losing a job is hurting 统领，后文列举心理与经济感受。
证据失业让人 financially terrifying、psychologically embarrassing，且感到不被需要、被排除在工作环境之外。
替换uneasy 概括了恐惧、尴尬和不安；比 insulted / enraged / guilty 更贴合整体情绪。

干扰项反证

Bembarrassing 与 not wanted 不等于核心感受是被侮辱。
C原文无愤怒情绪。
D原文没有说失业者自责或有罪感。
Q25推断题
答案 A

25. To which of the following would the author most probably agree?

  • AUnemployment benefits should not be made conditional.
  • BThe British welfare system indulges jobseekers’ laziness.
  • CThe jobseekers’ allowance has met their actual needs.
  • DOsborne’s reforms will reduce the risk of unemployment.
关键词作者观点定位：The principle of British welfare is no longer... unconditional payments；Instead... conditional。
定位定位 P4。作者用 But in Osborneland 转入批判，并对福利被改为 conditional 表示否定。
证据作者认为英国福利本应是对失业风险的保险，灾难发生时可得到 unconditional payments；现在却变成 conditional allowance。
替换should not be made conditional = receive unconditional payments / no longer that principle 是作者批判点。

干扰项反证

B这是 Osborne 对制度的指责，作者明显用讽刺语气反驳。
C£71.70 且 one of the least generous，说明未满足实际需要。
D作者没有认为改革会降低失业风险。

Q30｜P1-P6｜B

全文先指出美国法律职业的问题，再提出教育与所有权改革方案。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色加粗 marker = 题干关键词或原文同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。主旨题证据可分布在多个段落。

P1 S1All around the world, lawyers generate more hostility than the members of any other profession — with the possible exception of journalism. S2But there are few places where clients have more grounds for complaint than America.

P2 S1During the decade before the economic crisis, spending on legal services in America grew twice as fast as inflation. S2The best lawyers made skyscrapers-full of money, tempting ever more students to pile into law schools. S3But most law graduates never get a big-firm job. S4Many of them instead become the kind of nuisance-lawsuit filer that makes the tort system a costly nightmare.

P3 S1There are many reasons for this. S2One is the excessive costs of a legal education. S3There is just one path for a lawyer in most American states: a four-year undergraduate degree in some unrelated subject, then a three-year law degree at one of 200 law schools authorized by the American Bar Association and an expensive preparation for the bar exam. S4This leaves today’s average law-school graduate with $100,000 of debt on top of undergraduate debts. S5Law-school debt means that they have to work fearsomely hard.

P4 S1Reforming the system would help both lawyers and their customers. S2Sensible ideas have been around for a long time, but the state-level bodies that govern the profession have been too conservative to implement them. S3One idea is to allow people to study law as an undergraduate degree. S4Another is to let students sit for the bar after only two years of law school. S5If the bar exam is truly a stern enough test for a would-be lawyer, those who can sit it earlier should be allowed to do so. S6Students who do not need the extra training could cut their debt mountain by a third.

P5 S1The other reason why costs are so high is the restrictive guild-like ownership structure of the business. S2Except in the District of Columbia, non-lawyers may not own any share of a law firm. S3This keeps fees high and innovation slow. S4There is pressure for change from within the profession, but opponents of change among the regulators insist that keeping outsiders out of a law firm isolates lawyers from the pressure to make money rather than serve clients ethically.

P6 S1In fact, allowing non-lawyers to own shares in law firms would reduce costs and improve services to customers, by encouraging law firms to use technology and to employ professional managers to focus on improving firms’ efficiency. S2After all, other countries, such as Australia and Britain, have started liberalizing their legal professions. S3America should follow.

Q26细节题
答案 D

26. A lot of students take up law as their profession due to

  • Athe growing demand from clients.
  • Bthe increasing pressure of inflation.
  • Cthe prospect of working in big firms.
  • Dthe attraction of financial rewards.
关键词take up law → pile into law schools；due to → tempting。
定位定位 P2。题干 students take up law 对应原文 students pile into law schools。
证据最好的律师赚得盆满钵满，这种金钱前景诱使更多学生涌入法学院。
替换financial rewards = skyscrapers-full of money；take up law = pile into law schools。

干扰项反证

A客户需求并非学生入行原因。
Binflation 出现于法律服务支出增速，不是择业诱因。
Cbig-firm job 是多数毕业生得不到的结果，不是直接诱因。
Q27细节题
答案 D

27. Which of the following adds to the costs of legal education in most American states?

  • AHigher tuition fees for undergraduate studies.
  • BReceiving training by professional associations.
  • CAdmissions approval from the bar association.
  • DPursuing a bachelor’s degree in another major.
关键词costs of legal education → four-year undergraduate degree + three-year law degree。
定位定位 P3。题干 costs of legal education 与原文 excessive costs 原词呼应。
证据在多数州，成为律师先要读一个与法律无关的四年本科，再读三年法学院，导致高额债务。
替换pursuing a bachelor’s degree in another major = four-year undergraduate degree in some unrelated subject。

干扰项反证

A原文强调多读一个无关本科，不是本科阶段学费更高。
BABA 授权学校不是“由协会接受培训”。
C授权学校与入学批准不同，且不是主要增费点。
Q28细节题
答案 A

28. Hindrance to the reform of the legal system originates from

  • Athe rigid bodies governing the profession.
  • Blawyers’ and clients’ strong resistance.
  • Cthe stern exam for would-be lawyers.
  • Dnon-professionals’ sharp criticism.
关键词Hindrance / reform → state-level bodies too conservative。
定位定位 P4-P5。P4 直接说明改革理念存在已久，但管理职业的州级机构过于保守。
证据state-level bodies that govern the profession have been too conservative；P5 又说 regulators 中的改革反对者坚持排斥外人。

B客户是改革受益者，非阻力来源。
Cbar exam 是可改革制度的一部分，不是阻力源。

Q29细节题
答案 B

29. The guild-like ownership structure is considered “restrictive” partly because it

  • Aprevents lawyers from gaining due profits.
  • Bbans outsiders’ involvement in the profession.
  • Caggravates the ethical situation in the trade.
  • Dkeeps lawyers from holding law-firm shares.
关键词guild-like ownership structure / restrictive → non-lawyers may not own。
定位定位 P5。restrictive 后一句直接解释限制在哪里。
证据除哥伦比亚特区外，非律师不能拥有律所任何股份，这把外部人排除在法律行业所有权之外。
替换bans outsiders’ involvement = non-lawyers may not own any share of a law firm。

干扰项反证

A原文说高收费，未说阻止律师获得应得利润。
C反对者声称排斥外人是为了伦理，不能说它恶化伦理。
D限制的是 non-lawyers，不是 lawyers 持有股份。

30. In this text, the author mainly discusses

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色加粗 marker = 题干关键词或原文同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。主旨题证据可分布在多个段落。

P1 S1The US$3-million Fundamental Physics Prize is indeed an interesting experiment, as Alexander Polyakov said when he accepted this year’s award in March. S2And it is far from the only one of its type. S3As a News Feature article in Nature discusses, a string of lucrative awards for researchers have joined the Nobel Prizes in recent years. S4Many, like the Fundamental Physics Prize, are funded from the telephone-number-sized bank accounts of Internet entrepreneurs. S5These benefactors have succeeded in their chosen fields, they say, and they want to use their wealth to draw attention to those who have succeeded in science.

P2 S1What’s not to like? S2Quite a lot, according to a handful of scientists quoted in the News Feature. S3You cannot buy class, as the old saying goes, and these upstart entrepreneurs cannot buy their prizes the prestige of the Nobels. S4The new awards are an exercise in self-promotion for those behind them, say scientists. S5They could distort the achievement-based system of peer-review-led research. S6They could cement the status quo of peer-reviewed research. S7They do not fund peer-reviewed research. S8They perpetuate the myth of the lone genius.

P3S1The goals of the prize-givers seem as scattered as the criticism. S2Some want to shock, others to draw people into science, or to better reward those who have made their careers in research.

P4 S1As Nature has pointed out before, there are some legitimate concerns about how science prizes — both new and old — are distributed. S2The Breakthrough Prize in Life Sciences, launched this year, takes an unrepresentative view of what the life sciences include. S3But the Nobel Foundation’s limit of three recipients per prize, each of whom must still be living, has long been outgrown by the collaborative nature of modern research — as will be demonstrated by the inevitable row over who is ignored when it comes to acknowledging the discovery of the Higgs boson. S4The Nobels were, of course, themselves set up by a very rich individual who had decided what he wanted to do with his own money. S5Time, rather than intention, has given them legitimacy.

P5 S1As much as some scientists may complain about the new awards, two things seem clear. S2First, most researchers would accept such a prize if they were offered one. S3Second, it is surely a good thing that the money and attention come to science rather than go elsewhere. S4It is fair to criticize and question the mechanism — that is the culture of research, after all — but it is the prize-givers’ money to do with as they please. S5It is wise to take such gifts with gratitude and grace.

31. The Fundamental Physics Prize is seen as

  • Aa symbol of the entrepreneurs’ wealth.

  • Dan example of bankers’ investments.

32. The critics think that the new awards will most benefit

Q33例证题
答案 C

33. The discovery of the Higgs boson is a typical case which involves

  • Alegitimate concerns over the new prizes.
  • Bcontroversies over the recipients’ status.
  • Cthe joint effort of modern researchers.
  • Dthe demonstration of research findings.
关键词Higgs boson → collaborative nature of modern research。
定位定位 P4。Higgs boson 例子紧跟破折号，用来说明现代研究的协作性与三人获奖限制冲突。
证据诺奖每项最多三人的限制已被现代研究的合作本质超越，希格斯玻色子发现引发“谁被忽略”的争议正说明这一点。

Q34推断题
答案 B

34. According to Paragraph 4, which of the following is true of the Nobels?

  • AHistory has never cast doubt on them.
  • BTheir endurance has done justice to them.
  • CThey are the most representative honor.
  • DTheir legitimacy has long been in dispute.
关键词Nobels → Time... has given them legitimacy。
定位定位 P4 末句。题干 Nobels 原词复现。
证据诺奖也由富人个人创立，但“时间而非初衷赋予它们合法性”，说明长期存在让它们获得认可。
替换Their endurance has done justice to them = Time has given them legitimacy。

干扰项反证

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

P1 S1“The Heart of the Matter,” the just-released report by the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (AAAS), deserves praise for affirming the importance of the humanities and social sciences to the prosperity and security of liberal democracy in America. S2Regrettably, however, the report’s failure to address the true nature of the crisis facing liberal education may cause more harm than good.

P2 S1In 2010, leading congressional Democrats and Republicans sent letters to the AAAS asking that it identify actions that could be taken by “federal, state and local governments, universities, foundations, educators, individual benefactors and others” to “maintain national excellence in humanities and social scientific scholarship and education.” S2In response, the American Academy formed the Commission on the Humanities and Social Sciences. S3Among the commission’s 51 members are top-tier-university presidents, scholars, lawyers, judges, and business executives, as well as prominent figures from diplomacy, filmmaking, music and journalism.

P3 S1The goals identified in the report are generally admirable. S2Because representative government presupposes an informed citizenry, the report supports full literacy; stresses the study of history and government, particularly American history and American government; and encourages the use of new digital technologies. S3To encourage innovation and competition, the report calls for increased investment in research, the crafting of coherent curricula that improve students’ ability to solve problems and communicate effectively in the 21st century, increased funding for teachers and the encouragement of scholars to bring their learning to bear on the great challenges of the day. S4The report also advocates greater study of foreign languages, international affairs and the expansion of study abroad programs.

P4S1Unfortunately, despite 2½ years in the making, “The Heart of the Matter” never gets to the heart of the matter: the illiberal nature of liberal education at our leading colleges and universities. S2The commission ignores that for several decades America’s colleges and universities have produced graduates who don’t know the content and character of liberal education and are thus deprived of its benefits. S3Sadly, the spirit of inquiry once at home on campus has been replaced by the use of the humanities and social sciences as vehicles for publicizing “progressive,” or left-liberal propaganda.

P5 S1Today, professors routinely treat the progressive interpretation of history and progressive public policy as the proper subject of study while portraying conservative or classical liberal ideas — such as free markets and self-reliance — as falling outside the boundaries of routine, and sometimes legitimate, intellectual investigation.

P6S1The AAAS displays great enthusiasm for liberal education. S2Yet its report may well set back reform by obscuring the depth and breadth of the challenge that Congress asked it to illuminate.
