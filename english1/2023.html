彩色加粗 marker = 题干关键词或原文同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1 S1The weather in Texas may have cooled since the recent extreme heat, but the temperature will be high at the State Board of Education meeting in Austin this month as officials debate how climate change is taught in Texas schools.

P2S1Pat Hardy, who sympathises with the views of the energy sector, is resisting proposed changes to science standards for pre-teen pupils. S2“There are as many scientists working against all the panic of global climate change as there are those who are pushing it,” she claims. S3“Texas is an energy state and we need to recognise that. You need to remember where your bread is buttered.”

P3 S1Most scientists and experts sharply dispute Hardy’s views. S2Board members like her “casually dismiss the career work of scholars and scientists as just another misguided opinion,” says Dan Quinn, senior communications strategist at the Texas Freedom Network, a non-profit group that monitors public education.

P4S1Such debates reflect fierce discussions across the US, as researchers, policymakers, teachers and students step up demands for a greater focus on teaching about the facts of climate change in schools.

P5 S1A study last year by the National Center for Science Education, a non-profit group of scientists and teachers, looking at how state public schools across the country address climate change in science classes, gave barely half of US states a grade B+ or higher. S2Among the 10 worst performers were some of the most populous states, including Texas, which was given the lowest grade (F) and has a disproportionate influence because its textbooks are widely sold elsewhere.

P6 S1Glenn Branch, the centre’s deputy director, cautions that setting state-level science standards is only one limited benchmark in a country that decentralises decisions to local school boards. S2Even if a state is considered a high performer in its science standards, “that does not mean it will be taught,” he says.

P7S1Another issue is that while climate change is well integrated into some subjects and at some ages—such as earth and space sciences in high schools—it is not as well represented in curricula for younger children and in subjects that are more widely taught, such as biology and chemistry. S2It is also less prominent in many social studies courses.

P8 S1Branch points out that, even if a growing number of official guidelines and textbooks reflect scientific consensus on climate change, unofficial educational materials that convey more slanted perspectives are being distributed to teachers. S2They include materials sponsored by energy industry associations.

按“关键词—定位—证据—替换—排除”校验，不跨段臆断。

Q21修辞功能题
答案 C

In Paragraph 1, the weather in Texas is mentioned to

  • Aforecast a policy shift in Texas schools.
  • Bstress the consequences of climate change.
  • Cindicate the atmosphere at the board meeting.
  • Ddraw the public’s attention to energy shortages.
关键词weather in Texas → cooled / temperature will be high；board meeting → debate。
定位首段首句直接定位；weather 不是谈气象本身，而是借“温度高”转入会议争论。
证据“weather ... cooled ... but the temperature will be high at the State Board of Education meeting ... as officials debate ...”
替换atmosphere at the board meeting = temperature will be high + officials debate。

干扰项反证

Apolicy shift 只是可能的会议背景，原文没有预告政策将转向。
Bclimate change 是争论主题，不是 weather 句子的修辞目的。
Denergy shortages 未出现；energy sector 不等于能源短缺。

What does Quinn think of Hardy?

  • CShe shows no concern for pre-teens.
  • DShe expresses self-contradictory views.
关键词Quinn / Hardy → casually dismiss / career work of scholars and scientists。

证据Board members like her “casually dismiss the career work of scholars and scientists as just another misguided opinion.”
替换denies the value of scientific work = dismiss the career work ... as misguided opinion。

D原文没有指出 Hardy 自相矛盾。

  • Denvironmental teaching in some states lacks supervision.

P1 S1Communities throughout New England have been attempting to regulate short-term rentals since sites like Airbnb took off in the 2010s. S2Now, with record-high home prices and historically low inventory, there’s an increased urgency in such regulation, particularly among those who worry that developers will come in and buy up swaths of housing to flip for a fortune on the short-term rental market.

P2 S1In New Hampshire, where the rental vacancy rate has dropped below 1 percent, housing advocates fear unchecked short-term rentals will put further pressure on an already strained market. S2The state Legislature recently voted against a bill that would’ve made it illegal for towns to create legislation restricting short-term rentals.

P3 S1“We are at a crisis level on the supply of rental housing,” said Nick Taylor, executive director of the Workforce Housing Coalition of the Greater Seacoast. S2Without enough affordable housing in southern New Hampshire towns, “employers are having a hard time attracting employees, and workers are having a hard time finding a place to live,” Taylor said.

P4 S1However, short-term rentals also provide housing for tourists, pointed out Ryan Castle, CEO of a local association of realtors. S2“A lot of workers are servicing the tourist industry, and the tourism industry is serviced by those people coming in short term,” Castle said, “and so it’s a cyclical effect.”

P5 S1Short-term rentals themselves are not the crux of the issue, said Keren Horn, an expert on affordable housing policy. S2“I think individuals being able to rent out their second home is a good thing. If it’s their vacation home anyway, and it’s just empty, why can’t you make money off it?” Horn said. S3Issues arise, however, when developers attempt to create large-scale short-term rental facilities—de facto hotels—to bypass taxes and regulations. S4“I think the question is, shouldn’t a developer who’s really building a hotel, but disguising it as not a hotel, be treated and taxed and regulated like a hotel?” Horn said.

P6 S1At the end of 2018, Governor Charlie Baker of Massachusetts signed a bill to rein in those potential investor-buyers. S2The bill requires every rental host to register with the state, mandates they carry insurance, and opens the potential for local taxes on top of a new state levy. S3Boston took things even further, requiring renters to register with the city’s Inspectional Services Department.

P7 S1Horn said similar registration requirements could benefit struggling cities and towns, but “if we want to make a change in the housing market, the main one is we have to build a lot more.”

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色加粗 marker = 题干关键词或原文同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1 S1Scientific papers are the recordkeepers of progress in research. S2Each year researchers publish millions of papers in more than 30,000 journals. S3The scientific community measures the quality of those papers in a number of ways, including the perceived quality of the journal (as reflected by the title’s impact factor) and the number of citations a specific paper accumulates. S4The careers of scientists and the reputation of their institutions depend on the number and prestige of the papers they produce, but even more so on the citations attracted by these papers.

P2 S1Citation cartels, where journals, authors, and institutions conspire to inflate citation numbers, have existed for a long time. S2In 2016, researchers developed an algorithm to recognize suspicious citation patterns, including groups of authors that disproportionately cite one another and groups of journals that cite each other frequently to increase the impact factors of their publications. S3Recently, another expression of this predatory behavior has emerged: so-called support service consultancies that provide language and other editorial support to individual authors and to journals sometimes advise contributors to add a number of citations to their articles.

P3 S1The advent of electronic publishing and authors’ need to find outlets for their papers resulted in thousands of new journals. S2The birth of predatory journals wasn’t far behind. S3These journals can act as milk cows where every single article in an issue may cite a specific paper or a series of papers. S4In some instances, there is absolutely no relationship between the content of the article and the citations. S5The peculiar part is that the journal that the editor is supposedly working for is not profiting at all—it is just providing citations to other journals. S6Such practices can lead an article to accrue more than 150 citations in the same year that it was published.

P4 S1How insidious is this type of citation manipulation? S2In one example, an individual—acting as author, editor, and consultant—was able to use at least 15 journals as citation providers to articles published by five scientists at three universities. S3The problem is rampant in Scopus, a citation database, which includes a high number of the new “international” journals. S4In fact, a listing in Scopus seems to be a criterion to be targeted in this type of citation manipulation.

P5 S1Scopus itself has all the data necessary to detect this malpractice. S2Red flags include a large number of citations to an article within the first year. S3And for authors who wish to steer clear of citation cartel activities: when an editor, a reviewer, or a support service asks you to add inappropriate references, do not oblige and do report the request to the journal.

逐题证据链解析

按“关键词—定位—证据—替换—排除”校验，不跨段臆断。

Q36细节理解题
答案 B

According to Paragraph 1, the careers of scientists can be determined by

  • Ahow many citations their works contain.
  • Bhow many times their papers are cited.
  • Cthe prestige of the people they work with.
  • Dthe status they have in scientific circles.
关键词careers of scientists → citations attracted by these papers。
定位题干 Paragraph 1 + careers of scientists 直接定位 P1 末句。
证据The careers of scientists ... depend ... even more so on the citations attracted by these papers.
替换how many times their papers are cited = citations attracted by these papers。

干扰项反证

Aworks contain citations 指论文内引用了多少文献；原文说论文被别人引用多少次。
Cpeople they work with 未出现。
Dstatus 是结果概念，不是原文衡量指标。
Q37细节理解题
答案 C

The support service consultancies tend to

  • Arecommend journals to their clients.
  • Blist citation patterns for their clients.
  • Cask authors to include extra citations.
  • Dadvise contributors to cite each other.
关键词support service consultancies → advise contributors to add citations。
定位P2 末句出现题干原词 support service consultancies。
证据Support service consultancies ... sometimes advise contributors to add a number of citations to their articles.
替换ask authors to include extra citations = advise contributors to add a number of citations。

干扰项反证

A推荐期刊未出现。
Bcitation patterns 是研究者算法识别对象，不是咨询机构服务。
Dcite each other 是 citation cartels 的一种模式，不是 support consultancies 的具体动作。
Q38功能说明题
答案 A

The function of the “milk cow” journals is to

  • Aboost citation counts for certain authors.
  • Bhelp scholars publish articles at low cost.
  • Cinstruct first-time contributors in citation.
  • Dincrease the readership of new journals.
关键词milk cow journals → every article may cite a specific paper / accrue more than 150 citations。
定位P3 “milk cows” 后解释其操作方式和结果。
证据Every single article in an issue may cite a specific paper or series of papers; such practices can lead an article to accrue more than 150 citations in the same year.
替换boost citation counts = make many articles cite a specific paper。certain authors = owners of the targeted paper/series。

干扰项反证

Blow cost 未出现。
C不是教学引用，而是操纵引用。
D目标是 citations，不是 readership。
Q39细节推断题
答案 B

What can be learned about Scopus from the last two paragraphs?

  • AIt fosters competition among citation providers.
  • BIt has the capability to identify suspicious citations.
  • CIt hinders the growth of “international” journals.
  • DIt is established to prevent citation manipulation.
关键词Scopus → has all the data necessary to detect this malpractice。
定位last two paragraphs 中 P4 介绍 Scopus 被利用，P5 说明 Scopus 自身有检测数据。
证据Scopus itself has all the data necessary to detect this malpractice. Red flags include a large number of citations to an article within the first year.
替换capability to identify suspicious citations = data necessary to detect malpractice + red flags。

干扰项反证

Acompetition among providers 未出现。
C它包含许多 new “international” journals，不是阻碍其增长。
DScopus 是 citation database，不是为防操纵而建立。
