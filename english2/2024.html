Q21—Q25。题干定位词、原文定位词、作答证据、同义替换全部闭环。

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q21｜P1 Coyle 新书观点

new book + economy growing → gains more evenly shared → C。

Q22｜P2 digital technologies

Paragraph 2 + greater use → boost productivity → C。

Q23｜P3 fear + social buy-in

transformative technologies → roadblock / need social buy-in → D。

Q24｜P5—P6 城市例证

eight cities / 15 cities → particularly concentrated → A。

Q25｜P6—P8 concern → response

as Coyle suggests → broaden digital imaginations → D。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词 / 同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1 1In her new book Cogs and Monsters: What Economics Is, and What It Should Be, Diane Coyle, an economist at Cambridge University, argues that the digital economy requires new ways of thinking about progress. 2“Whatever we mean by the economy growing, by things getting better, the gains will have to be more evenly shared than in the recent past,” she writes. 3“An economy of tech millionaires or billionaires and gig workers, with middle-income jobs undercut by automation, will not be politically sustainable.”

P2 Q22 段落定位 1Improving living standards and increasing prosperity for more people will require greater use of digital technologies to boost productivity in various sectors, including health care and construction, says Coyle. 2But people can’t be expected to embrace the changes if they’re not seeing the benefits—if they’re just seeing good jobs being destroyed.

P3 1In a recent interview, Coyle said she fears that tech’s inequality problem could be a roadblock to deploying AI. 2“We’re talking about disruption,” she says. “These are transformative technologies that change the ways we spend our time every day, that change business models that succeed.” 3To make such “tremendous changes,” she adds, you need social buy-in.

P4 1Instead, says Coyle, resentment is simmering among many as the benefits are perceived to go to elites in a handful of prosperous cities.

P5 1According to the Brookings Institution, a short list of eight American cities that included San Francisco, San Jose, Boston, and Seattle had roughly 38% of all tech jobs by 2019. 2New AI technologies are particularly concentrated: Brookings’ Mark Muro and Sifan Liu estimate that just 15 cities account for two-thirds of the AI assets and capabilities in the United States.

P6 1The dominance of a few cities in the invention and commercialization of AI means that geographical disparities in wealth will continue to soar. 2Not only will this foster political and social unrest, but it could, as Coyle suggests, hold back the sorts of AI technologies needed for regional economies to grow.

P7 1Part of the solution could lie in somehow loosening the stranglehold that Big Tech has on defining the AI agenda. 2That will likely take increased federal funding for research independent of the tech giants.

P8 1A more immediate response is to broaden our digital imaginations to conceive of AI technologies that don’t simply replace jobs but expand opportunities in the sectors that different parts of the country care most about, like health care, education, and manufacturing.

逐题证据链解析

每题只保留“关键词—定位—证据—替换—排除”。

Q21细节理解题
答案 C

Coyle argues in her new book that economic growth should ________.

  • [A]give rise to innovations
  • [B]diversify career choices
  • [C]benefit people equally
  • [D]be promoted forcefully
关键词题干 Coyle / new book / economic growth → 原文 “Coyle”“new book”“the economy growing”。
定位由 Coyle + new book 定到 P1，再看她对 “the economy growing / gains” 的判断。
证据“the gains will have to be more evenly shared than in the recent past”。
替换benefit people equally ≈ gains more evenly shared；题干 economic growth ≈ 原文 the economy growing。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A“new ways of thinking”不是“产生创新”；原文谈增长收益分配，不谈创新来源。
Bgig workers 和 middle-income jobs 是反面背景，不是“职业选择多样化”。
C原文明确说 gains 要 more evenly shared，范围与选项最贴合。
D“requires new ways of thinking”不等于强力推动经济增长，forcefully 无依据。
Q22细节理解题
答案 C

According to Paragraph 2, digital technologies should be used to ________.

  • [A]bring about instant prosperity
  • [B]reduce people’s workload
  • [C]raise overall work efficiency
  • [D]enhance cross-sector cooperation
关键词Paragraph 2 → P2 段首锚点；digital technologies / used → 原文 “digital technologies”“greater use”。
定位按段落题先锁 P2；顺读第一句，to 后目的直接给出 “boost productivity”。
证据“greater use of digital technologies to boost productivity in various sectors”。
替换raise overall work efficiency ≈ boost productivity；overall / various sectors 对应跨多个部门。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

Aprosperity 是目标背景，但不是 digital technologies 的直接用途；“instant”更无原文支持。
B原文说 boost productivity，不说减少工作量；自动化毁工作是风险，不是用途。
Cboost productivity 与 raise work efficiency 是直接同义替换。
Dhealth care and construction 是举例领域，不是强调跨部门合作。
Q23推断细节题
答案 D

What does Coyle fear about transformative technologies?

  • [A]They may affect work-life balance.
  • [B]They may be impractical to deploy.
  • [C]They may incur huge expenditure.
  • [D]They may be unwelcome to the public.
关键词Coyle / fear / transformative technologies → P3 原词复现 “Coyle”“fears”“transformative technologies”。
定位定位到 P3 后看 fear 的原因和结果：inequality problem 会成为部署 AI 的 roadblock；重大变化需要 social buy-in。
证据“tech’s inequality problem could be a roadblock to deploying AI” + “you need social buy-in”。
替换unwelcome to the public ≈ lack of social buy-in；fear about deployment 的阻力来自公众不买账。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A“change the ways we spend our time”看似涉及生活，但不是 fear 的核心，也不是 work-life balance。
Broadblock 不是技术不可行，而是 inequality / social buy-in 的社会阻力。
C本段没有 expenditure / cost 线索。
Dsocial buy-in 是公众接受；需要它说明可能不被公众接受，推断幅度合适。
Q24例证目的题
答案 A

Several American cities are mentioned to show ________.

  • [A]the uneven distribution of AI technologies in the US
  • [B]the disappointing prospect of tech jobs in the US
  • [C]the fast progress of US regional economies
  • [D]the increasing significance of US AI assets
关键词Several American cities → 原文 “eight American cities”；mentioned/show → 后文总结 “particularly concentrated / dominance of a few cities”。
定位城市名单是例子；例子后的概括句才是答案：AI assets and capabilities concentrated in a few cities。
证据“New AI technologies are particularly concentrated... just 15 cities account for two-thirds of the AI assets and capabilities”。
替换uneven distribution ≈ particularly concentrated / dominance of a few cities。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A例子和概括句共同指向 AI 技术/资产集中在少数城市，最稳。
Btech jobs 数字是背景，不是“前景令人失望”；AI assets 才是论点。
C原文说地区财富差距会继续扩大，不是区域经济快速进步。
DAI assets 被提到是为说明集中度，不是说明其重要性增加。
Q25作者建议题
答案 D

With regard to Coyle’s concern, the author suggests ________.

  • [A]raising funds to start new AI projects
  • [B]encouraging collaboration in AI research
  • [C]guarding against the side effects of AI
  • [D]redefining the role of AI technologies
关键词Coyle’s concern → P6 “as Coyle suggests”；author suggests → P8 “A more immediate response is”。
定位先用 Coyle’s concern 定到 P6，再顺读解决方案。P7 是远期条件，P8 给更直接建议。
证据“broaden our digital imaginations to conceive of AI technologies that don’t simply replace jobs but expand opportunities”。
替换redefining the role of AI technologies ≈ conceive of AI technologies not as job replacers but as opportunity expanders。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

AP7 提到 federal funding for research independent of tech giants，不是 start new AI projects。
Bindependent research 不等于 collaboration；原文没有鼓励合作。
C不是泛泛防范 AI 副作用，而是重新想象 AI 服务不同地区、扩大机会。
D“don’t simply replace jobs but expand opportunities”正是在重新定义 AI 的角色。

Q26—Q30。题干定位词、原文定位词、作答证据、同义替换全部闭环。

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q26｜P1 UK needs

called for urgent action → stable supply / less imports → A。

Q27｜P2 fresh incentives

don’t go far enough / fail to promote → A。

Q28｜P2 因果句

not investing in domestic supply → exposed to prices → B。

Q29｜P3 causes

farmers / landowners hesitation → D。

Q30｜P4—P5 Goodall 建议

government support + productive tree planting + greater impetus → C。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词 / 同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1 Q26 段落定位 1The UK is facing a future construction crisis because of a failure to plant trees to produce wood, Confor has warned. 2The forestry and wood trade body has called for urgent action to reduce the country’s reliance on timber imports and provide a stable supply of wood for future generations. 3Currently only 20 per cent of the UK’s wood requirement is home-grown while it remains the second-largest net importer of timber in the world.

P2 1Coming at a time of fresh incentives from the UK government for landowners to grow more trees, the trade body says these don’t go far enough and fail to promote the benefits of planting them to boost timber supplies. 2“Not only are we facing a carbon crisis now, but we will also be facing a future construction crisis because of a failure to plant trees to produce wood,” said Stuart Goodall, chief executive of Confor. 3“For decades we have not taken responsibility for investing in our domestic wood supply, leaving us exposed to fluctuating prices and fighting for future supplies of wood as global demand rises and our own supplies fall.”

P3 1The UK has ideal conditions for growing wood to build low-carbon homes and is a global leader in certifying that its forests are sustainably managed, Confor says. 2While around three quarters of Scottish homes are built from Scottish timber, the use of home-grown wood in England is only around 25 per cent. 3The causes of the UK’s current position are complex and range from outdated perceptions of productive forestry to the decimation of trees by grey squirrels. 4It also encompasses significant hesitation on behalf of farmers and other landowners to invest in longer-term planting projects.

P4 1While productive tree planting can deliver real financial benefits to rural economies and contribute to the UK’s net-zero strategy, the focus of government support continues to be on food production and the rewilding and planting of native woodland solely for biodiversity. 2Goodall added: “While food production and biodiversity health are clearly of critical importance, we need our land to also provide secure supplies of wood for construction, manufacturing and contributing to net zero.”

P5 1“While the UK government has stated its ambition for more tree planting, there has been little action on the ground. 2Confor is now calling for much greater impetus behind those aspirations to ensure we have enough wood to meet increasing demand.”

逐题证据链解析

每题只保留“关键词—定位—证据—替换—排除”。

Q26段落细节题
答案 A

It can be learned from Paragraph 1 that the UK needs to ________.

  • [A]increase its domestic wood supply
  • [B]reduce its demand for timber
  • [C]lower its wood production costs
  • [D]lift its control on timber imports
关键词Paragraph 1 → P1 锚点；the UK → 原文 “The UK”；needs to → 原文 “called for urgent action”。
定位先锁 P1；Confor 的 warning 后面紧跟 “called for urgent action to...” 给出需要做什么。
证据“reduce the country’s reliance on timber imports and provide a stable supply of wood”。
替换increase domestic wood supply ≈ provide a stable supply of wood / reduce reliance on imports。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A“stable supply of wood”与“domestic / home-grown”背景合起来，正是增加本土木材供给。
B原文要减少对进口的依赖，不是减少木材需求。
C成本没有出现。
D原文不是放松进口管制，而是降低进口依赖。
Q27细节判断题
答案 A

According to Confor, the UK government’s fresh incentives ________.

  • [A]can hardly address a construction crisis
  • [B]are believed to come at a wrong time
  • [C]seem to be misleading for landowners
  • [D]will be too costly to put into practice
关键词Confor → 原文 “the trade body / chief executive of Confor”；fresh incentives → 原文同词复现。
定位定位 P2 首句；fresh incentives 后的评价词 “don’t go far enough / fail to...” 是答案核心。
证据“these don’t go far enough and fail to promote the benefits of planting them to boost timber supplies”。
替换can hardly address a construction crisis ≈ don’t go far enough / fail to promote planting enough to boost supply。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A“不够、未能促进”说明这些激励难以应对未来建筑危机，表述最稳。
Bcoming at a time of fresh incentives 只是时间背景，不是“时机错误”。
C没有说误导 landowners，只说激励力度不足。
D没有成本过高的内容。
Q28因果细节题
答案 B

The UK’s exposure to fluctuating wood prices is a result of ________.

  • [A]the government’s inaction on timber imports
  • [B]inadequate investment in growing wood
  • [C]the competition among timber traders at home
  • [D]wood producers’ motive to maximise profits
关键词题干 exposure to fluctuating wood prices → 原文 “exposed to fluctuating prices”；result of → 前半句 “not taken responsibility for investing...”。
定位同一句内找因果：not taken responsibility for investing in domestic supply → leaving us exposed。
证据“not taken responsibility for investing in our domestic wood supply, leaving us exposed to fluctuating prices”。
替换inadequate investment in growing wood ≈ not taken responsibility for investing in domestic wood supply。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A问题不是“timber imports 上的不作为”，而是本土木材供应投资不足。
B因果链最直接：未投资国内木材供应 → 暴露于价格波动。
C没有国内木材商竞争的线索。
D没有生产者逐利动机的线索。
Q29原因细节题
答案 D

Which of the following causes the shortage of wood supply in the UK?

  • [A]Excessive timber consumption in construction.
  • [B]Unfavourable conditions for growing wood.
  • [C]Outdated technologies of the wood industry.
  • [D]Farmers’ unwillingness to plant trees.
关键词shortage of wood supply → 原文 “current position / supplies fall”；causes → P3 原词 “causes”。
定位定位 P3 的 causes 句；列举中与选项能精确对应的是 farmers and landowners 的 hesitation。
证据“significant hesitation on behalf of farmers and other landowners to invest in longer-term planting projects”。
替换Farmers’ unwillingness to plant trees ≈ hesitation ... to invest in longer-term planting projects。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

Aconstruction 需要木材，但原文没有说“过度消费”导致短缺。
B原文明确说 UK has ideal conditions，正好反驳 unfavourable conditions。
Coutdated perceptions 不是 outdated technologies。
Dfarmers / landowners hesitation 对应 unwillingness to plant trees。
Q30观点建议题
答案 C

What does Goodall think the UK government should do?

  • [A]Subsidise the building of low-carbon homes.
  • [B]Pay greater attention to boosting rural economies.
  • [C]Provide more support for productive tree planting.
  • [D]Give priority to pursuing its net-zero strategy.
关键词Goodall → P4 引语；government → 原文 “government support”；应做什么 → productive tree planting 与 greater impetus。
定位先定 Goodall 的直接引语，再看前后对政府支持重点的批评：目前支持偏食物/生态，应该给生产性植树更多推动。
证据“productive tree planting can deliver...” + “government support continues to be on...” + “much greater impetus behind those aspirations”。
替换Provide more support for productive tree planting ≈ government support should give greater impetus to productive tree planting。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

Alow-carbon homes 是木材用途之一，不是 Goodall 要政府补贴的对象。
Brural economies 是 productive planting 的收益之一，不是政策焦点本身。
C全文末段 “greater impetus” 与 P4 “productive tree planting”合在一起支持该项。
Dnet-zero 是共同收益，但原文说 food/biodiversity/net-zero 都重要，核心仍是 secure wood supplies / productive planting。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词 / 同义定位表达；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1 Q31 段落定位 1One of the biggest challenges in keeping unsafe aging drivers off the road is convincing them that it’s time to turn over the keys. 2“It’s a complete life-changer” when someone stops—or is forced to stop—driving, said former risk manager Anne M. Menke.

P2 1“The American Medical Association advises physicians that ‘in situations where clear evidence of substantial driving impairment implies a strong threat to patient and public safety, and where the physician’s advice to discontinue driving privilege is ignored, it is desirable and ethical to notify the Department of Motor Vehicles,’” Menke wrote. 2Some states require physicians to report, others allow but do not mandate reports, while a few consider a report a breach of confidentiality. 3There could be liability and penalties if a physician does not act in accordance with state laws on reporting and confidentiality,” she counseled.

P3 1Part of the problem in keeping older drivers safe is that the difficulties are addressed piecemeal by different professions with different focuses, including gerontologists, highway administration officials, automotive engineers and others, said gerontologist Elizabeth Dugan. 2“There’s not a National Institute of Older Driver Studies,” she said. 3“We need better evidence on what makes drivers unsafe” and what can help, said Dugan.

P4 1One thing that does seem to work is requiring drivers to report in person for license renewal. 2Mandatory in-person renewal was associated with a 31 per cent reduction in fatal crashes involving drivers 85 or older, according to one study. 3Passing vision tests also produced a similar decline in fatal crashes for those drivers, although there appeared to be no benefit from combining the two.

P5 1Many older drivers don’t see eye doctors or can’t afford to. 2Primary care providers have their hands full and may not be able to follow through with patients who have trouble driving because they can’t turn their heads or remember where they are going—or have gotten shorter and haven’t changed their seat settings sufficiently to reach car pedals easily.

P6 1As long as there are other cars on the roads, self-driving cars won’t solve the problems of crashes, said Dugan. 2Avoiding dangers posed by all those human drivers would require too many algorithms, she said. 3But we need to do more to improve safety, said Dugan. 4“If we’re going to have 100-year lives, we need cars that a 90-year-old can drive comfortably.”

Q31段落细节题
答案 C

According to Paragraph 1, keeping unsafe aging drivers off the road ________.

  • [A]is a new safety measure
  • [B]has become a disputed issue
  • [C]can be a tough task to complete
  • [D]will be beneficial to their health

证据“One of the biggest challenges ... is convincing them that it’s time to turn over the keys.”
替换tough task to complete ≈ one of the biggest challenges / convincing them is difficult。

干扰项反证｜先给最强理由，再看原文是否支持

A文中没有说这是新的安全措施。
B有州法律差异在 P2，但 P1 不是争议 issue。
Cbiggest challenges 与 tough task 是最直接替换。

Q32细节理解题

B各州规定不同，不等于普遍认为不现实。
C有些州要求上报，反而说明没有被广泛认为不必要。

According to Dugan, efforts to keep older drivers safe ________.

Q35观点建议题
答案 B

Dugan thinks that the solution to the problems of crashes may lie in ________.

  • [A]upgrading self-driving vehicles
  • [B]developing senior-friendly cars
  • [C]renovating transport facilities
