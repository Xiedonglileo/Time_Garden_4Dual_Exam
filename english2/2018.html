完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

P11It is curious that Stephen Koziatek feels almost as though he has to justify his efforts to give his students a better future.

P22Mr. Koziatek is part of something pioneering. He is a teacher at a New Hampshire high school where learning is not something of books and tests and mechanical memorization, but practical. 3When did it become accepted wisdom that students should be able to name the 13th president of the United States but be utterly overwhelmed by a broken bike chain?

P34As Koziatek knows, there is learning in just about everything. Nothing is necessarily gained by forcing students to learn geometry at a graffitied desk stuck with generations of discarded chewing gum. 5They can also learn geometry by assembling a bicycle.

P46But he’s also found a kind of insidious prejudice. 7Working with your hands is seen as almost a mark of inferiority. 8Schools in the family of vocational education “have that stereotype...that it’s for kids who can’t make it academically,” he says.

P5Q23 段落定位9On one hand, that viewpoint is a logical product of America’s evolution. Manufacturing is not the economic engine that it once was. 10The job security that the US economy once offered to high school graduates has largely evaporated. 11More education is the new principle. We want more for our kids, and rightfully so.

P612But the headlong push into bachelor’s degrees for all — and the subtle devaluing of anything less — misses an important point: That’s not the only thing the American economy needs. 13Yes, a bachelor’s degree opens more doors. But even now, 54 percent of the jobs in the country are middle-skill jobs, such as construction and high-skill manufacturing. But only 44 percent of workers are adequately trained.

P714In other words, at a time when the working class has turned the country on its political head, frustrated that the opportunity that once defined America is vanishing, one obvious solution is staring us in the face. 15There is a gap in working-class jobs, but the workers who need those jobs most aren’t equipped to do them. 16Koziatek’s Manchester School of Technology High School is trying to fill that gap.

P817Koziatek’s school is a wake-up call. When education becomes one-size-fits-all, it risks overlooking a nation’s diversity of gifts.

Q21例证题
答案 A

A broken bike chain is mentioned to show students’ lack of ________.

  • [A]practical ability
  • [B]academic training
  • [C]pioneering spirit
  • [D]mechanical memorization
关键词题干 broken bike chain → 原文同词；lack of 对应 utterly overwhelmed。
定位定位到 P2 反问句：会背总统却被坏自行车链难住。
证据students should be able to name... but be utterly overwhelmed by a broken bike chain。
替换practical ability = 处理 broken bike chain 的动手能力。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[B]academic training 反而是他们已有的书本记忆。
[C]pioneering spirit 形容 Koziatek 的教育尝试，不是学生缺少的能力。
[D]mechanical memorization 是被批评的传统学习，不是 broken bike chain 所体现的缺口。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P11To combat the trap of putting a premium on being busy, Cal Newport, author of Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World, recommends building a habit of “deep work” — the ability to focus without distraction.

P22There are a number of approaches to mastering the art of deep work — be it lengthy retreats dedicated to a specific task; developing a daily ritual; or taking “journalistic” approach to seizing moments of deep work when you can throughout the day. 3Whichever approach, the key is to determine your length of focus time and stick to it.

P34Newport also recommends “deep scheduling” to combat constant interruptions and get more done in less time. 5“At any given point, I should have deep work scheduled for roughly the next month. Once on the calendar, I protect this time like I would a doctor’s appointment or important meeting,” he writes.

P46Another approach to getting more done in less time is to rethink how you prioritise your day — in particular how we craft our to-do lists. Tim Harford, author of Messy: The Power of Disorder to Transform Our Lives, points to a study in the early 1980s that divided undergraduates into two groups: some were advised to set out monthly goals and study activities; others were told to plan activities and goals in much more detail, day by day.

P57While the researchers assumed that the well-structured daily plans would be most effective when it came to the execution of tasks, they were wrong: the detailed daily plans demotivated students. Harford argues that the inevitable distractions often render the daily to-do list ineffective, while leaving room for improvisation in such a list can reap the best results.

P68In order to make the most of our focus and energy, we also need to embrace downtime, or as Newport suggests, “be lazy”. 9“Idleness is not just a vacation, an indulgence or a vice; it is as indispensable to the brain as vitamin D is to the body...[idleness] is, paradoxically, necessary to getting any work done,” he argues.

P710Srini Pillay, an assistant professor of psychiatry at Harvard Medical School, believes this counterintuitive link between downtime and productivity may be due to the way our brains operate. 11When our brains switch between being focused and unfocused on a task, they tend to be more efficient.

Q36细节题
答案 A

The key to mastering the art of deep work is to ________.

  • [A]keep to your focus time
  • [B]list your immediate tasks
  • [C]make specific daily plans
  • [D]seize every minute to work
关键词deep work 原词定位；key 原词定位。
定位P2 最后一句直接给 key。
证据the key is to determine your length of focus time and stick to it。
替换keep to your focus time = determine... and stick to it。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[B]任务清单属于后文另一个方法，非 deep work 的 key。
[C]详细日计划后文被反证。
[D]seize moments 不等于每分钟都工作，且要 stick to focus time。
Q37例证题
答案 D

The study in the early 1980s cited by Harford shows that ________.

  • [A]distractions may actually increase efficiency
  • [B]daily schedules are indispensable to studying
  • [C]students are hardly motivated by monthly goals
  • [D]detailed plans may not be as fruitful as expected
关键词study in the early 1980s 原词定位；结果在下一段 they were wrong。
定位研究者以为详细日计划最有效，结果反而让学生失去动力。
证据they were wrong: the detailed daily plans demotivated students。
替换not as fruitful as expected = researchers assumed effective but were wrong。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]distractions render to-do list ineffective，不是提高效率。
[B]daily schedules 不可或缺与原文相反。
[C]被否定的是 detailed daily plans，不是 monthly goals。
Q38细节题
答案 D

According to Newport, idleness is ________.

  • [A]a desirable mental state for busy people
  • [B]a major contributor to physical health
  • [C]an effective way to save time and energy
  • [D]an essential factor in accomplishing any work
关键词idleness 原词定位；Newport 引语。
定位P6 明确说 idleness 对大脑不可或缺，是完成任何工作的必要条件。
证据idleness... is, paradoxically, necessary to getting any work done。
替换essential factor = indispensable / necessary。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]desirable mental state 过泛；不是 busy people 的心理状态。
[B]vitamin D 是类比，不是主要贡献身体健康。
[C]省时间精力不是原文。
Q39细节题
答案 B

Pillay believes that our brains’ shift between being focused and unfocused ________.

  • [A]can result in psychological well-being
  • [B]can bring about greater efficiency
  • [C]is aimed at better balance in work
  • [D]is driven by task urgency
关键词Pillay + focused/unfocused 原词定位。
定位P7 直接说明切换时大脑更有效率。
证据When our brains switch between being focused and unfocused on a task, they tend to be more efficient。
替换greater efficiency = more efficient。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]心理幸福感未出现。
[C]不是目的 aim，而是运作方式。
[D]task urgency 未出现。
