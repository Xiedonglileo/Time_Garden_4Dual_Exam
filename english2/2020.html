Q21—Q25。题干定位词、原文定位词、作答证据、同义替换全部闭环。

答案速查：21A　22D　23B　24C　25D

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q21｜实验目的

Quinn + tested → rats can detect social signals from robotic rats.

Q22｜asocial robot 行为

asocial robot → simply moved forwards/backwards and side to side.

Q23｜释放社会机器人原因

Quinn + return the favour + direct reciprocity.

Q24｜Wiles 评论

minimal design + head/tail/features/scent not necessary.

Q25｜全文结论

finding shows how sensitive rats are to social cues.

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1Q21 段落定位1Rats and other animals need to be highly attuned to social signals from others so they can identify friends to cooperate with and enemies to avoid. 2To find out if this extends to non-living beings, Laleh Quinn at the University of California, San Diego, and her colleagues tested whether rats can detect social signals from robotic rats.

P23They housed eight adult rats with two types of robotic rat—one social and one asocial—for four days. 4The robot rats were quite minimalist, resembling a chunkier version of a computer mouse with wheels to move around and colourful markings.

P3Q22 实验定位5During the experiment, the social robot rat followed the living rats around, played with the same toys, and opened cage doors to let trapped rats escape. 6Meanwhile, the asocial robot simply moved forwards and backwards and side to side.

P47Next, the researchers trapped the robots in cages and gave the rats the opportunity to release them by pressing a lever. 8Across 18 trials each, the living rats were 52 per cent more likely on average to set the social robot free than the asocial one. 9This suggests that the rats perceived the social robot as a genuine social being. 10They may have bonded more with the social robot because it displayed behaviors like communal exploring and playing. This could lead to the rats better remembering having freed it earlier, and wanting the robot to return the favour when they get trapped, says Quinn.

P511“Rats have been shown to engage in multiple forms of reciprocal help and cooperation, including what is referred to as direct reciprocity—where a rat will help another rat that has previously helped them,” says Quinn.

P612The readiness of the rats to befriend the social robot was surprising given its minimal design. 13The robot was the same size as a regular rat but resembled a simple plastic box on wheels. 14“We’d assumed we’d have to give it a moving head and tail, facial features, and put a scent on it to make it smell like a real rat, but that wasn’t necessary,” says Janet Wiles at the University of Queensland in Australia, who helped with the research.

P7Q25 全文结论15The finding shows how sensitive rats are to social cues, even when they come from basic robots. 16Similarly, children tend to treat robots as if they are fellow beings, even when they display only simple social signals. 17“We humans seem to be fascinated by robots, and it turns out other animals are too,” says Wiles.

Q21细节题
答案 A

Quinn and her colleagues conducted a test to see if rats can ________.

  • [A]pick up social signals from non-living rats.
  • [B]distinguish a friendly rat from a hostile one.
  • [C]attain sociable traits through special training.
  • [D]send out warning messages to their fellows.
关键词Quinn and her colleagues → Laleh Quinn ... and her colleagues；test → tested whether；non-living rats → robotic rats。
定位首段末句直接给实验目的，顺序切片停在 tested whether 后。
证据rats can detect social signals from robotic rats。
替换pick up social signals = detect social signals；non-living rats = robotic rats。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[B]friendly/hostile 似乎对应 friends/enemies，但那是动物社会信号的背景，不是本实验检测对象。
[C]special training 原文没有；实验是观察识别信号，不是训练获得社会性。
[D]warning messages 属于无中生有；原文只谈 detect social signals。
Q22细节题
答案 D

What did the asocial robot do during the experiment?

  • [A]It followed the social robot.
  • [B]It played with some toys.
  • [C]It set the trapped rats free.
  • [D]It moved around alone.
关键词asocial robot 原词复现；during the experiment → P3 实验过程。
定位P3 先写 social robot 的动作，再用 Meanwhile 转入 asocial robot。
证据the asocial robot simply moved forwards and backwards and side to side。
替换moved forwards and backwards and side to side = moved around；simply/alone 概括其单独移动、无互动。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]followed 是 social robot 的动作，不是 asocial robot。
[B]played with toys 也是 social robot 的动作。
[C]opened cage doors / let trapped rats escape 是 social robot 的动作。
Q23因果推断题
答案 B

According to Quinn, the rats released the social robot because they ________.direct reciprocity

  • [A]tried to practice a means of escape.
  • [B]expected it to do the same in return.
  • [C]wanted to display their intelligence.
  • [D]considered that an interesting game.
关键词Quinn 原词定位；释放原因落在 “wanting the robot to return the favour” 与 “direct reciprocity”。
定位P4 解释释放社会机器人的原因，P5 Quinn 用 reciprocal help 补强。
证据wanting the robot to return the favour when they get trapped；direct reciprocity—where a rat will help another rat that has previously helped them。
替换do the same in return = return the favour / direct reciprocity。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]pressing a lever 是释放方式，不是释放原因。
[C]intelligence 原文未评价；不能从按杠杆推出“展示智力”。

Q24观点推断题
答案 C

Janet Wiles notes that rats ________.minimal design

  • [A]can remember other rats’ facial features.
  • [B]differentiate smells better than sizes.
  • [C]respond more to actions than to looks.
  • [D]can be scared by a plastic box on wheels.
关键词Janet Wiles 原词定位；minimal design 与 “wasn’t necessary” 是判断点。
定位P6 说机器人外形简陋；原本以为需要头尾、面部、气味，但事实不需要。
证据We’d assumed we’d have to give it a moving head and tail, facial features, and put a scent on it ... but that wasn’t necessary。
替换not necessary 的外形/气味特征反推：起作用的是 social robot 的互动行为，而非 looks。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]facial features 是研究者预设需求，并未说老鼠记住其他老鼠面部。
[B]scent 被否定为不必要，不能推出嗅觉强弱比较。
[D]plastic box on wheels 描述外观，不表示老鼠害怕。

答案 D

It can be learned from the text that rats ________. It can be learned from the text that rats ________.Q25 全文结论

  • [A]appear to be adaptable to new surroundings.
  • [B]are more socially active than other animals.
  • [C]behave differently from children in socializing.

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P11It is true that CEO pay has gone up — top ones may make 300 times the pay of typical workers on average, and since the mid-1970s, CEO pay for large publicly traded American corporations has, by varying estimates, gone up by about 500%. 2The typical CEO of a top American corporation now makes about $18.9 million a year.

P23The best model for understanding the growth of CEO pay is that of limited CEO talent in a world where business opportunities for the top firms are growing rapidly. 4The efforts of America’s highest-earning 1% have been one of the more dynamic elements of the global economy. 5It’s not popular to say, but one reason their pay has gone up so much is that CEOs really have upped their game relative to many other workers in the U.S. economy.

P36Q27 前后对比Today’s CEO, at least for major American firms, must have many more skills than simply being able to “run the company.” 7CEOs must have a good sense of financial markets and maybe even how the company should trade in them. 8They also need better public relations skills than their predecessors, as the costs of even a minor slipup can be significant. 9Then there’s the fact that large American companies are much more globalized than ever before, with supply chains spread across a larger number of countries. To lead in that system requires knowledge that is fairly mind-boggling. 10Plus, virtually all major American companies are becoming tech companies, often with their own research and development. 11And beyond this, major CEOs still have to do all the day-to-day work they have always done.

P412The common idea that high CEO pay is mainly about ripping people off doesn’t explain history very well. 13By most measures, corporate governance has become a lot tighter and more rigorous since the 1970s. 14Yet it is principally during this period of stronger governance that CEO pay has been high and rising. 15That suggests it is in the broader corporate interest to recruit top candidates for increasingly tough jobs.

P516Furthermore, the highest CEO salaries are paid to outside candidates, not to the cozy insider picks, another sign that high CEO pay is not some kind of depredation at the expense of the rest of the company. 17And the stock market reacts positively when companies tie CEO pay to, say, stock prices, a sign that those practices build up corporate value not just for the CEO. 18High CEO pay is repeatedly explained as a way to recruit scarce talent for increasingly tough, value-building jobs, not merely as overpayment.

Q38词义题
答案 D

The word “assuage” (Line 9, Para. 2) is closest in meaning to ________.

  • [A]deepen.
  • [B]define.
  • [C]maintain.
  • [D]relieve.
关键词assuage 原词定位；上下文为 booming economy 与 anxious urgency 的关系。
定位经济好转却 “done little to assuage” 焦虑，说明 assuage 是缓解/减轻。
证据The booming economy seems to have done little to assuage this underlying generational sense of anxious urgency。
替换relieve = assuage；缓解焦虑。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]deepen 与“缓解”相反。
[B]define 是定义，不符合经济好转对焦虑的作用。
[C]maintain 是维持，也不符合 done little to 的语义。
