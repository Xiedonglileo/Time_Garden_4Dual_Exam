Q21—Q25。题干定位词、原文定位词、作答证据、同义替换全部闭环。

答案速查：21A　22C　23D　24B　25A

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q21｜nowadays homework

recent years → particularly scorned.

Q22｜L.A. Unified rule

poor students may have difficulty completing homework.

Q23｜policy problem

10% grade makes skipping homework easy.

Q24｜thorny question

whether homework matters academically.

Q25｜title

policy is faulty, put on hold.

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1Q21 段落定位1Homework has never been terribly popular with students and even many parents, but in recent years it has been particularly scorned. 2School districts across the country, most recently Los Angeles Unified, are revising their thinking on this educational ritual. 3Unfortunately, L.A. Unified has produced an inflexible policy which mandates that with the exception of some advanced courses, homework may no longer count for more than 10% of a student’s academic grade.

P24This rule is meant to address the difficulty that students from impoverished or chaotic homes might have in completing their homework. 5But the policy is unclear and contradictory. 6Certainly, no homework should be assigned that students cannot complete on their own or that they cannot do without expensive equipment. 7But if the district is essentially giving a pass to students who do not do their homework because of complicated family lives, it is going riskily close to the implication that standards need to be lowered for poor children.

P3Q23 段落定位8District administrators say that homework will still be a part of schooling; teachers are allowed to assign as much of it as they want. 9But with homework counting for no more than 10% of their grades, students can easily skip half their homework and see very little difference on their report cards. 10Some students might do well on state tests without completing their homework, but what about the students who performed well on the tests and did their homework? 11It is quite possible that the homework helped. 12Yet rather than empowering teachers to find what works best for their students, the policy imposes a flat, across-the-board rule.

P4Q24 段落定位13At the same time, the policy addresses none of the truly thorny questions about homework. 14If the district finds homework to be unimportant to its students’ academic achievement, it should move to reduce or eliminate the assignments, not make them count for almost nothing. Conversely, if homework matters, it should account for a significant portion of the grade. 15Meanwhile, this policy does nothing to ensure that the homework students receive is meaningful or appropriate to their age and the subject, or that teachers are not assigning more than they are willing to review and correct.

P5Q25 主旨定位16The homework rules should be put on hold while the school board, which is responsible for setting educational policy, looks into the matter and conducts public hearings. 17It is not too late for L.A. Unified to do homework right.

定位闭环补全层

A suitable title for this text could be ______.

Q21推断细节题
答案 A

It is implied in Paragraph 1 that nowadays homework ______.

  • [A]is receiving more criticism
  • [B]is gaining more preferences
  • [C]is no longer an educational ritual
  • [D]is not required for advanced courses
关键词Paragraph 1 → P1；nowadays → in recent years；criticism → scorned。
定位P1 首句先说 homework 一直不受欢迎，再用 but / particularly scorned 加强到“近年来更受批评”。
证据in recent years it has been particularly scorned.
替换receiving more criticism = being particularly scorned。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[B]preferences 与 scorned 方向相反。
[C]educational ritual 仍被称为 ritual，并非不再是。
[D]advanced courses 是政策例外，不是 homework 普遍不要求。

Q24段落细节题
答案 B

As mentioned in Paragraph 4, a key question unanswered about homework is whether ______.unimportant to its students’ academic achievement

  • [A]it should be eliminated
  • [B]it counts much in schooling
  • [C]it places extra burdens on teachers
  • [D]it is important for grades
关键词Paragraph 4 → P4；key question → truly thorny questions；counts much in schooling → important to academic achievement / homework matters。

Q25标题题
答案 A

A suitable title for this text could be ______. A suitable title for this text could be ______.Q25 主旨定位

  • [A]A Faulty Approach to Homework
  • [B]A Welcomed Policy for Poor Students
  • [C]Thorny Questions about Homework
  • [D]Wrong Interpretations of an Educational Policy
关键词标题题综合全文：LA 作业政策 unclear, contradictory, flat, across-the-board，应 put on hold。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P11Pretty in pink: adult women do not remember being so obsessed with the colour, yet it is pervasive in our young girls’ lives. 2It is not that pink is intrinsically bad, but it is such a tiny slice of the rainbow and, though it may celebrate girlhood in one way, it also repeatedly and firmly fuses girls’ identity to appearance. 3Then it presents that connection, even among two-year-olds, between girls as not only innocent but as evidence of innocence. 4Looking around, I despaired at the singular lack of imagination about girls’ lives and interests.

P2Q27 段落定位5Girls’ attraction to pink may seem unavoidable, somehow encoded in their DNA, but according to Jo Paoletti, an associate professor of American Studies, it is not. 6Children were not colour-coded at all until the early 20th century: in the era before domestic washing machines all babies wore white as a practical matter, since the only way of getting clothes clean was to boil them. 7What’s more, both boys and girls wore what were thought of as gender-neutral dresses. 8When nursery colours were introduced, pink was actually considered the more masculine colour, a pastel version of red, which was associated with strength. Blue, with its intimations of the Virgin Mary, constancy and faithfulness, symbolised femininity. 9It was not until the mid-1980s, when amplifying age and sex differences became a dominant children’s marketing strategy, that pink fully came into its own, when it began to seem inherently attractive to girls, part of what defined them as female, at least for the first few critical years.

P310I had not realised how profoundly marketing trends dictated our perception of what is natural to kids, including our core beliefs about their psychological development. 11Take the toddler. 12I assumed that phase was something experts developed after years of research into children’s behaviour: wrong. 13Turns out, according to Daniel Cook, a historian of childhood consumerism, it was popularised as a marketing trick by clothing manufacturers in the 1930s.

P4Q29 段落定位14Trade publications counselled department stores that, in order to increase sales, they should create a “third stepping stone” between infant wear and older kids’ clothes. 15It was only after “toddler” became a common shoppers’ term that it evolved into a broadly accepted developmental stage. 16Splitting kids, or adults, into ever-tinier categories has proved a sure-fire way to boost profits. 17And one of the easiest ways to segment a market is to magnify gender differences — or invent them where they did not previously exist.

P1Q31 段落定位1In 2010, a federal judge shook America’s biotech industry to its core. 2Companies had won patents for isolated DNA for decades — by 2005 some 20% of human genes were patented. 3But in March 2010 a judge ruled that genes were unpatentable. 4Executives were violently agitated. 5The Biotechnology Industry Organisation (BIO), a trade group, assured members that this was just a “preliminary step” in a longer battle.

P26On July 29th they were relieved, at least temporarily. 7A federal appeals court overturned the prior decision, ruling that Myriad Genetics could indeed hold patents to two genes that help forecast a woman’s risk of breast cancer. 8The chief executive of Myriad, a company in Utah, said the ruling was a blessing to firms and patients alike.

P39But as companies continue their attempts at personalised medicine, the courts will remain rather busy. 10The Myriad case itself is probably not over. 11Critics make three main arguments against gene patents: a gene is a product of nature, so it may not be patented; gene patents suppress innovation rather than reward it; and patents’ monopolies restrict access to genetic tests such as Myriad’s. 12A growing number seem to agree. 13Last year a federal task-force urged reform for patents related to genetic tests. 14In October the Department of Justice filed a brief in the Myriad case, arguing that an isolated DNA molecule “is no less a product of nature...than are cotton fibres that have been separated from cotton seeds.”

P415Despite the appeals court’s decision, big questions remain unanswered. 16For example, it is unclear whether the sequencing of a whole genome violates the patents of individual genes within it, or whether diagnostic uses of genes can be kept away from patients and researchers by a single patent holder.

P517Industry lawyers insist that patents are not being sought for life itself. 18Hans Sauer, a lawyer for BIO, says firms are eager to protect discoveries that connect genes with illnesses. 19What companies want, he argues, are patents for establishing useful correlations between genes and diseases, not simple ownership of human DNA.

P620The debate has become a central issue for researchers, doctors, lawyers and business groups. 21At recent meetings on the future of genetic testing, each meeting was packed, reflecting how widely gene patenting worried those involved. 22For now, the courts have supplied temporary answers, but the larger controversy over gene patenting remains unsettled.

P2Q36/Q37 段落定位4No one tries harder than the jobless to find silver linings in this national economic disaster. 5Many said that unemployment, while extremely painful, had improved them in some ways: they had become less materialistic and more financially prudent; they were more aware of the struggles of others. 6In limited respects, perhaps the recession will leave society better off. 7At the very least, it has awoken us from our national fever dream of easy riches and bigger houses, and put a necessary end to an era of reckless personal spending.

P38But for the most part, these benefits seem thin, uncertain, and far off. 9In The Moral Consequences of Economic Growth, the economic historian Benjamin Friedman argues that both inside and outside the U.S., lengthy periods of economic stagnation or decline have almost always left society more mean-spirited and less inclusive, and have usually stopped or reversed the advance of rights and freedoms. 10Anti-immigrant sentiment typically increases, as does conflict between races and classes.

P411Income inequality usually falls during a recession, but it has not shrunk in this one. 12Indeed, this period of economic weakness may reinforce class divides, and decrease opportunities to cross them — especially for young people. 13The research of Till Von Wachter, the economist at Columbia University, suggests that not all people graduating into a recession see their life chances dimmed: those with degrees from elite universities catch up fairly quickly to where they otherwise would have been if they had graduated in better times; it is the masses beneath them that are left behind.

P514In the Internet age, it is particularly easy to see the resentment that has always been hidden within American society. 15More difficult, in the moment, is discerning precisely how these lean times are affecting society’s character. 16In many respects, the U.S. was more socially tolerant entering this recession than at any time in its history, and a variety of national polls on social conflict since then have shown mixed results. 17We will have to wait and see exactly how these hard times will reshape our social fabric. 18But they certainly will reshape it, and all the more so the longer they extend.

Q36词组句义题
答案 D

By saying “to find silver linings” (Line 1, Para. 2) the author suggests that the jobless try to ______.Q36/Q37 段落定位

  • [A]seek subsidies from the government
  • [B]make profits from the troubled economy
  • [C]explore reasons for the unemployment
  • [D]look on the bright side of the recession
关键词silver linings 原文定位；后文 improved them in some ways 解释其含义。
定位P2 说失业者努力在经济灾难中寻找积极面，随后列举变得更节俭、更理解他人。
证据unemployment... had improved them in some ways.
替换look on the bright side = find silver linings。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]政府补贴未出现。
[B]不是从危机中赚钱。
[C]不是分析失业原因。

Q39细节题
答案 D

The research of Till Von Wachter suggests that in the recession graduates from elite universities tend to ______.

  • [A]lag behind the others due to decreased opportunities
  • [B]catch up quickly with experienced employees
  • [C]see their life chances as dimmed as the others’
  • [D]recover more quickly than the others
关键词Till Von Wachter 原词定位；elite universities 原文复现。
定位P4 明确对比 elite graduates 与 masses：前者很快追上，后者落后。
证据those with degrees from elite universities catch up fairly quickly...; it is the masses beneath them that are left behind.
替换recover more quickly than the others = catch up fairly quickly while others are left behind。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]落后的是 masses beneath them，不是 elite graduates。
[B]不是追上 experienced employees，而是追上若好时期毕业本该有的位置。
[C]not all people... life chances dimmed 排除。
Q40推断态度题
答案 C

The author thinks that the influence of hard times on society is ______.

  • [A]trivial
  • [B]positive
  • [C]certain
  • [D]destructive
关键词hard times 原词定位到末段；influence → reshape our social fabric。
定位作者说具体怎样影响还要等，但确定会重塑社会，且时间越长影响越大。
证据But they certainly will reshape it, and all the more so the longer they extend.
替换certain = certainly will reshape it。

干扰项反证｜先 steelman，再回到原文边界

[A]trivial 与 reshape society 相反。
[B]全文提到有限积极面，但主旨不说影响是积极。
[D]负面很多，但末句问影响是否确定，destructive 过度绝对。
