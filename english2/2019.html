Text 1｜高分阅读路径图解析

Q21—Q25。guilt 的道德功能：从“负担”到“社会胶水”。

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q21｜细节题

P1：guilt 与儿童社会/道德规范成长相伴，帮助学会道歉、安抚他人与良心。

Q22｜细节题

P2：popular imagination 中 guilt 是 weighted with stones 的情绪负担。

Q23｜推断题

P2：Vaish 认为 guilt 重新被理解，源于对情绪非二元、情境作用不同的认识。

Q24｜细节题

P4：Malti 等人证明 guilt 与 sympathy 是合作分享的不同路径。

Q25｜词义题

P5—P6：moral transgressions 后出现 guilt，下一段解释为 caused harm and regret。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1Q21 段落定位Unlike so-called basic emotions such as sadness, fear, and anger, guilt emerges a little later, in conjunction with a child’s growing grasp of social and moral norms. Children aren’t born knowing how to say “I’m sorry”; rather, they learn over time that such statements appease parents and friends — and their own consciences. This is why researchers generally regard so-called moral guilt, in the right amount, to be a good thing.

P2Q22 段落定位Q23 Vaish 定位In the popular imagination, of course, guilt still gets a bad rap. It is deeply uncomfortable — it’s the emotional equivalent of wearing a jacket weighted with stones. Yet this understanding is outdated. “There has been a kind of revival or a rethinking about what guilt is and what role guilt can serve,” says Amrisha Vaish, a psychology researcher at the University of Virginia, adding that this revival is part of a larger recognition that emotions aren’t binary — feelings that may be advantageous in one context may be harmful in another. Jealousy and anger, for example, may have evolved to alert us to important inequalities. Too much happiness can be destructive.

P3And guilt, by prompting us to think more deeply about our goodness, can encourage humans to make up for errors and fix relationships. Guilt, in other words, can help hold a cooperative species together. It is a kind of social glue.

P4Q24 Malti 定位Viewed in this light, guilt is an opportunity. Work by Tina Malti, a psychology professor at the University of Toronto, suggests that guilt may compensate for an emotional deficiency. In a number of studies, Malti and others have shown that guilt and sympathy may represent different pathways to cooperation and sharing. Some kids who are low in sympathy may make up for that shortfall by experiencing more guilt, which can rein in their nastier impulses. And vice versa: High sympathy can substitute for low guilt.

P5Q25 词义定位In a 2014 study, for example, Malti looked at 244 children. Using caregiver assessments and the children’s self-observations, she rated each child’s overall sympathy level and his or her tendency to feel negative emotions after moral transgressions. Then the kids were handed chocolate coins, and given a chance to share them with an anonymous child. For the low-sympathy kids, how much they shared appeared to turn on how inclined they were to feel guilty. The guilt-prone ones shared more, even though they hadn’t magically become more sympathetic to the other child’s deprivation.

P6“That’s good news,” Malti says. “We can be prosocial because we caused harm and we feel regret.”

逐题证据链解析

每题按“题干关键词—原文定位—证据—替换—排除”闭环。

Q21细节题
答案 C

Researchers think that guilt can be a good thing because it may help ______.Q21 段落定位

  • [A] regulate a child’s basic emotions
  • [B] improve a child’s intellectual ability
  • [C] foster a child’s moral development
  • [D] intensify a child’s positive feelings
关键词Researchers → P1 researchers；guilt / a good thing 原词复现；help 对应 P1 learn over time 与 social and moral norms。
定位题干定位词全部落在 P1；顺着 “This is why” 回看前一句儿童如何学习道歉与良心约束。
证据P1 说 guilt 随着 child’s growing grasp of social and moral norms 出现，儿童逐渐学会道歉、安抚他人和自己的良心。
替换growing grasp of social and moral norms / consciences = moral development；foster = help grow。

干扰项反证

A文中说 guilt 不是 basic emotions，未说调节基本情绪。
Bintellectual ability 与智力提升无证据。
Dguilt 不是 positive feelings；文中也未说强化积极情绪。
Q22细节题
答案 B

According to Paragraph 2, many people still consider guilt to be ______.

  • [A] deceptive
  • [B] burdensome
  • [C] addictive
  • [D] inexcusable
关键词Paragraph 2 → P2 段首锚点；guilt 原词复现；many people 对应 popular imagination。
定位按段落定位到 P2；bad rap 后面的比喻句给出公众印象。
证据P2 称 guilt deeply uncomfortable，像穿着 weighted with stones 的夹克。
替换weighted with stones / uncomfortable = burdensome。

干扰项反证

Q23推断题
答案 D

Vaish holds that the rethinking about guilt comes from an awareness that ______.

  • [A] emotions are context-independent
  • [B] emotions are socially constructive
  • [C] emotional stability can benefit health
  • [D] an emotion can play opposing roles
关键词Vaish 原词复现；rethinking about guilt 对应 revival or a rethinking；awareness 对应 larger recognition。
定位在 P2 Vaish 引语后找 adding that 引出的解释；破折号后是关键判断。
证据Vaish 说 emotions aren’t binary，某种情绪在一个情境中有利，在另一个情境中有害。
替换advantageous in one context / harmful in another = play opposing roles。

干扰项反证

A原文正说情绪依赖语境，A 反向。
B社会建设性只适用于 guilt 的部分作用，不能概括 Vaish 的 awareness。
Chealth 未出现，属无中生有。
Q24细节题
答案 B

Malti and others have shown that cooperation and sharing ______.Tina Malti

  • [A] may help correct emotional deficiencies
  • [B] can result from either sympathy or guilt
  • [C] can bring about emotional satisfaction
  • [D] may be the outcome of impulsive acts
关键词Malti and others 原词复现；cooperation and sharing 原词复现。
定位定位 P4 “have shown that” 后面的宾语从句，答案就在同一句。
证据P4 直接说 guilt and sympathy may represent different pathways to cooperation and sharing。
替换different pathways to cooperation and sharing = cooperation and sharing can result from either sympathy or guilt。

干扰项反证

Cemotional satisfaction 未出现。

Q25词义题
答案 D

The word “transgressions” (Line 4, Para.5) is closest in meaning to ______.Q25 词义定位

  • [A] teachings
  • [B] discussions
  • [C] restrictions
  • [D] wrongdoings
关键词transgressions 原词复现；moral 与后文 caused harm / regret 构成语义线索。
定位看 P5 词前的 moral 与词后的 feel negative emotions；再用 P6 “caused harm and feel regret” 回扣。
证据P6 说 “We can be prosocial because we caused harm and we feel regret.” 说明 transgressions 是造成伤害、引发后悔的行为。
替换moral transgressions = moral wrongdoings。

干扰项反证

Ateachings 是教导，不会自然导致 guilt。
Bdiscussions 只是讨论，与 moral guilt 不匹配。
Crestrictions 是限制，不是犯错行为。

Q26—Q30。森林碳汇管理：短期降低吸碳能力，长期增强韧性。

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q26｜推断题

P1：harder challenges 的解释是森林未来可能从吸碳者变成排碳者。

Q27｜细节题

P2：为了长期 carbon sinks，现在可能要降低吸碳能力。

Q28｜细节题

P3：Forest Carbon Plan aims to thin out young trees and clear brush。

Q29｜细节题

P5：资源有限，所以必须优先处理火灾或干旱风险最高区域。

Q30｜态度题

P2 + P7：leading the way / should serve as a model 表明支持。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1Q26 段落定位Forests give us shade, quiet and one of the harder challenges in the fight against climate change. Even as we humans count on forests to soak up a good share of the carbon dioxide we produce, we are threatening their ability to do so. The climate change we are hastening could one day leave us with forests that emit more carbon than they absorb.

P2Q27 段落定位Thankfully, there is a way out of this trap — but it involves striking a subtle balance. Helping forests flourish as valuable “carbon sinks” long into the future may require reducing their capacity to absorb carbon now. California is leading the way, as it does on so many climate efforts, in figuring out the details.

P3Q28 计划定位The state’s proposed Forest Carbon Plan aims to double efforts to thin out young trees and clear brush in parts of the forest. This temporarily lowers carbon-carrying capacity. But the remaining trees draw a greater share of the available moisture, so they grow and thrive, restoring the forest’s capacity to pull carbon from the air. Healthy trees are also better able to fend off insects. The landscape is rendered less easily burnable. Even in the event of a fire, fewer trees are consumed.

P4The need for such planning is increasingly urgent. Already, since 2010, drought and insects have killed over 100 million trees in California, most of them in 2016 alone, and wildfires have burned hundreds of thousands of acres.

P5Q29 段落定位California plans to treat 35,000 acres of forest a year by 2020, and 60,000 by 2030 — financed from the proceeds of the state’s emissions-permit auctions. That’s only a small share of the total acreage that could benefit, about half a million acres in all, so it will be vital to prioritize areas at greatest risk of fire or drought.

P6The strategy also aims to ensure that carbon in woody material removed from the forests is locked away in the form of solid lumber or burned as biofuel in vehicles that would otherwise run on fossil fuels. New research on transportation biofuels is already underway.

P7Q30 态度定位State governments are well accustomed to managing forests, but traditionally they’ve focused on wildlife, watersheds and opportunities for recreation. Only recently have they come to see the vital part forests will have to play in storing carbon. California’s plan, which is expected to be finalized by the governor next year, should serve as a model.

Q31—Q35。美国农业劳工短缺：移民规则、H-2A 签证与进口选择。

题目顺序与原文证据分布

Q31｜细节题

P1—P2：farm labor shortages 的根因指向 immigration rules / agricultural worker visa。

Q32｜细节题

P3：agricultural workforce 正在 settled、married、aging，其中 aging 是选项对应。

Q33｜细节题

P3：oft-debated cure 是让 Native U.S. workers 回农场，但作者称不现实。

Q34｜细节题

P5—P6：H-2A visa 程序繁琐且延误导致工人平均晚到 22 天。

Q35｜主旨标题题

全文：农业劳工短缺、移民规则、签证低效，结尾落到 import food or labor。

完整原文｜定位词与证据原位标注

彩色 marker = 题干定位词；粗下划线 + Q 标签 = 作答证据。

P1Q31 段落范围American farmers have been complaining of labor shortages for several years. The complaints are unlikely to stop without an overhaul of immigration rules for farm workers.

P2Congress has obstructed efforts to create a more straightforward visa for agricultural workers that would let foreign workers stay longer in the U.S. and change jobs within the industry. If this doesn’t change, American businesses, communities, and consumers will be the losers.

P3Q32 劳动力定位Perhaps half of U.S. farm laborers are undocumented immigrants. As fewer such workers enter the country, the characteristics of the agricultural workforce are changing. Today’s farm laborers, while still predominantly born in Mexico, are more likely to be settled rather than migrating and more likely to be married than single. They’re also aging. At the start of this century, about one-third of crop workers were over the age of 35. Now more than half are. And picking crops is hard on older bodies. One oft-debated cure for this labor shortage remains as implausible as it’s been all along: Native U.S. workers won’t be returning to the farm.

P4Q33 方案定位Mechanization isn’t the answer, either — not yet, at least. Production of corn, cotton, rice, soybeans, and wheat has been largely mechanized, but many high-value, labor-intensive crops, such as strawberries, need labor. Even dairy farms, where robots do a small share of milking, have a long way to go before they’re automated.

P5Q34 H-2A 定位As a result, farms have grown increasingly reliant on temporary guest workers using the H-2A visa to fill the gaps in the workforce. Starting around 2012, requests for the visas rose sharply; from 2011 to 2016 the number of visas issued more than doubled.

P6The H-2A visa has no numerical cap, unlike the H-2B visa for nonagricultural work, which is limited to 66,000 a year. Even so, employers complain they aren’t given all the workers they need. The process is cumbersome, expensive, and unreliable. One survey found that bureaucratic delays led the average H-2A worker to arrive on the job 22 days late. The shortage is compounded by federal immigration raids, which remove some workers and drive others underground.

P7In a 2012 survey, 71 percent of tree-fruit growers and almost 80 percent of raisin and berry growers said they were short of labor. Some western farmers have responded by moving operations to Mexico. From 1998 to 2000, 14.5 percent of the fruit Americans consumed was imported. Little more than a decade later, the share of imports was 25.8 percent.

P8Q35 标题定位In effect, the U.S. can import food or it can import the workers who pick it.
